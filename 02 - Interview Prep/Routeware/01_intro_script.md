# 2-Minute Intro — Routeware Senior .NET Developer

> Use this for **every round**. Memorize the **shape**, not the words. If you sound like you're reciting, slow down and re-engage eye contact.

---

## The Frame (memorize this skeleton)
1. **Who I am now** (1 sentence)
2. **Current work + one concrete win** (2–3 sentences)
3. **Arc — what I've owned across 6 years** (2–3 sentences)
4. **Why this role specifically** (1–2 sentences — echo JD language)
5. **Hand back** (1 sentence)

---

## The Script (~110 seconds spoken at a natural pace)

> "Hi, I'm Akash — I'm a full-stack software engineer with **6+ years** working primarily in **C# and .NET**, building **API-first backend services** and integration platforms.
>
> Right now at **Energy Exemplar**, I'm the engineer behind the Import/Export integration subsystem for **PLEXOS Cloud**. I designed it as a **reusable NuGet service library** with a clean interface contract — and that pattern was adopted by **multiple product teams**, which is something I'm proud of because it set a shared standard rather than letting each team fork their own version. I also built a cross-platform CLI and a Vue.js Excel Add-In on top of the same library, which proved the contract worked across very different surfaces.
>
> Before that, at **eGain**, I led the modernization of the analytics platform — migrating from **SSAS to AWS Redshift**. I redesigned the data pipelines, refactored the data-access layer, and we landed a **>30% query performance improvement** with zero data loss in the cutover. That work is closest in spirit to the modernization theme I saw in your JD.
>
> Earlier, at **IKS Health**, I hardened the auth layer on a **HIPAA-adjacent clinical platform** — engineering a **JWT-based authentication module** that closed critical security vulnerabilities in document-management services.
>
> What drew me to this role specifically is the combination you mentioned in the JD — **modernizing long-standing applications while balancing stability and customer value**. That's exactly the work I've been doing, and the WinForms / VB.NET modernization angle is something I'd be genuinely excited to dig into.
>
> Happy to go deeper on any of that."

## Variants (pick by interviewer)

### For Round 1 (AI screen)
Keep it tight — **90 seconds max**. AI graders favor structure + keywords. Lead with: ".NET, C#, API-first, microservices, AWS, modernization." Drop the IKS paragraph if pressed for time.

### For Round 2 (US team member)
Use the full script. Americans value the **arc + why-this-role** ending. Land the "modernization theme I saw in your JD" line — it signals you actually read it.

### For Round 3 (Bangalore team member)
Compress slightly. Add a closing sentence: *"I'm strongest on the backend and integration side, comfortable across the full stack, and I work primarily in C#/.NET."* Indian technical rounds want to know exactly what you're claiming so they can probe it.

- "modernization"
- "reusable service pattern"
- "clean interface contract"
- "performance improvement" (with number)
- "security-critical" / "HIPAA-adjacent"
- "long-standing applications"

---

## What NOT to say
- ❌ "I'm passionate about coding" — generic, signals junior
- ❌ "I'm looking for new challenges" — empty
- ❌ "I have 6 years of experience in many technologies" — vague
- ❌ Don't apologize for the 6 vs 7 year gap — never mention it
- ❌ Don't list every framework you've touched — depth > breadth in intros

## After the Intro — anticipated follow-ups

**"Tell me more about the NuGet library."** → Story C (90s).
**"What was the hardest part of the Redshift migration?"** → Validation: comparing aggregates row-by-row across old/new pipelines without disrupting live SSRS reports.
**"Why are you leaving Energy Exemplar?"** → Honest, neutral: "Looking for a senior role with more architectural ownership across the platform — Routeware's modernization scope is exactly that."
**"What's your strongest skill?"** → "Designing clean interface contracts for libraries and APIs that other teams consume. That's what I did at EE and it's what I enjoy most."
**"What's a weakness?"** → "I tend to over-invest in the first abstraction. I've learned to ship the concrete version first and extract the abstraction when a second caller actually shows up." (This is a real, technical, non-cliché answer — interviewers love it.)
